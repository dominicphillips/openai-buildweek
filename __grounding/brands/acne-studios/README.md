# Acne Studios

Research snapshot: 2026-07-17. This is independent product grounding, not an endorsement, affiliation, or style-replication brief.

## What it is

Acne Studios is a Stockholm fashion house that grew out of the multidisciplinary ACNE creative collective established in 1996. The [official brand account](https://www.acnestudios.com/apac/en/about/about.html) identifies Jonny Johansson as founder and creative director and describes fashion as one expression within a wider interest in photography, art, architecture, and contemporary culture. Sources differ in how they enumerate the original collective's co-founders, so this fashion-focused dossier does not collapse every collective credit into a single founding claim. The house remains active under Johansson.

## Designer and role

**Jonny Johansson** is the founder and continuing creative director foregrounded by Acne Studios. A 2025 [Vogue profile](https://www.vogue.com/article/you-need-to-sacrifice-a-lot-to-be-good-acne-studioss-jonny-johansson-on-the-power-of-control) confirms his ongoing role and describes a deliberately controlled, long-horizon approach to the brand. A separate [Vogue Business history](https://www.vogue.com/article/acne-studios-ceo-on-turning-the-scandi-brand-into-a-paris-staple) situates the fashion house within the earlier creative collective.

## Documented process and point of view

The official description emphasizes juxtaposition, exacting detail, tailoring, eclectic references, and custom-developed fabrics. That gives the product a useful method: place a controlled silhouette beside an unexpected material, image, or cultural reference, then resolve the contrast through construction rather than explanation. Recent collection notes, including the [Fall/Winter 2025 press release](https://www.showstudio.com/cms/documents/3801/6.Acne_Studios_FW25_Press_release.pdf), continue to frame garments through material experimentation and altered familiar forms.

Neutral vocabulary for product analysis:

- spare tailoring interrupted by an eccentric surface;
- elongated, relaxed, or slightly off-balance proportion;
- custom textile as the conceptual center;
- lived-in denim beside polished leather;
- soft structure and ambiguous formality;
- art, interiors, and photography used as research inputs rather than decoration to trace.

## Product-use notes

Carry forward as editable questions:

- Should the idea live primarily in silhouette, textile, fastening, or finish?
- What happens when one polished element is paired with one visibly aged element?
- Can a reference from furniture, photography, or architecture be translated into rhythm or volume instead of printed literally?
- Which detail makes a relaxed object feel intentional rather than unresolved?

Do **not** copy the face logo, Musubi knot construction, proprietary textile artwork, named denim washes, exact patterns, or campaign composition. Translate references into fastening location and load, straightness, ease, textile density, contrast, and finish. A source image's photographic license also does not grant rights to reproduce the garment design shown in it.
