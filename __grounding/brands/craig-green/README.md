# Craig Green

Research snapshot: 2026-07-17. This is independent product grounding, not an endorsement, affiliation, or style-replication brief.

## Designer and documented point of view

**Craig Green** is the London label's founder and designer. The [official archive](https://www.craig-green.com/collections) records the collections as a continuous practice; museum sources show familiar workwear and tailoring reorganized through quilting, straps, panels, and structures that can read as protection, burden, or movement.

Neutral transferable traits:

- begin with a durable workwear block and change its structure for a stated reason;
- use quilting to define volume, articulation, or protection rather than as surface pattern alone;
- let straps and extensions show forces, attachment, and possible movement;
- test a sculptural gesture against dressing, walking, storage, and repair.

## Product-use notes

Carry forward a map of load, attachment, articulation, and removal. Prototype the garment without the sculptural extension first, then prove that every added element changes motion or meaning. Do not copy a flag structure, strap layout, quilt map, runway staging, or exact silhouette.
