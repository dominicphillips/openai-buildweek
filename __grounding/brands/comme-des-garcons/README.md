# Comme des Garçons

Research snapshot: 2026-07-17. This is independent product grounding, not an endorsement, affiliation, or style-replication brief.

## Designer and documented point of view

**Rei Kawakubo** founded Comme des Garçons and is the designer of its principal womenswear line. The Met's [*Art of the In-Between* overview](https://www.metmuseum.org/exhibitions/listings/2017/rei-kawakubo) documents a practice that challenges conventional beauty, taste, and fashionability; its [gallery guide](https://www.metmuseum.org/exhibitions/listings/2017/rei-kawakubo/exhibition-galleries) organizes the work through unstable pairs such as fashion/antifashion, self/other, and clothes/not clothes.

Neutral transferable traits:

- question one assumed binary before drawing the garment;
- treat negative space and body clearance as design materials;
- allow asymmetry, incompletion, or wear to carry meaning when construction supports it;
- judge a conceptual gesture by the new question it makes visible, not by novelty alone.

## Product-use notes

State the convention being questioned, build a wearable baseline, then change one spatial or construction rule and document the result. Do not reproduce known lumps-and-bumps placements, distressed knits, runway objects, polka-dot/heart marks, or a recognizable Kawakubo silhouette.
