# Vetements

Research snapshot: 2026-07-17. This is independent product grounding, not an endorsement, affiliation, or style-replication brief.

## What it is

Vetements is a fashion house formed in 2014 by brothers Guram and Demna Gvasalia with a wider design collective. Its early work used familiar wardrobe categories—hoodies, bombers, jeans, printed T-shirts—and changed their scale, construction, context, or social charge. The house remains active; its [official site](https://www.vetements.com/) identifies Guram Gvasalia as creative director, and the [Fédération de la Haute Couture et de la Mode](https://www.fhcm.paris/en/house/vetements) lists the house in its current directory.

## Documented process and point of view

An early [British Vogue interview](https://www.vogue.co.uk/article/vetements-interview) describes a process that began with an ordinary archetype—such as a bomber—and asked what change would make it newly desirable. The result was less about inventing a garment category than redirecting attention through proportion, styling, construction, and context. The 2015 interview documents a Demna-era process; Guram's appointment begins a later period of named creative direction, and current authorship should not be folded back into Demna's period.

Neutral vocabulary for product analysis:

- amplified or collapsed scale;
- wardrobe archetypes displaced into an unexpected context;
- exposed joins, reconstruction, and visible material history;
- blunt graphics used as social signal;
- awkward length, dropped balance, and deliberate anti-polish;
- familiar utility made theatrical through proportion.

These are descriptive traits, not a prompt to generate “in the style of Vetements.”

## Product-use notes

Carry forward as editable questions:

- Which familiar garment category should remain recognizable?
- What single proportion change alters posture or movement?
- Can an existing seam, wear mark, or panel become the basis for reconstruction?
- What happens when an everyday object is moved into a new context without pretending it is rare?

Do **not** copy the DHL mark, branded collaborations, proprietary graphics, exact runway patterns, signature slogans, or a recognizable Vetements silhouette. Convert references into parameters such as sleeve-to-body ratio, hem position, panel rotation, surface wear, and degree of finish. Preserve the source link and record which parameter changed.
