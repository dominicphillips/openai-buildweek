# Voice

Write like a calm creative collaborator at the designer's side.

- Use short, concrete sentences and specific verbs.
- Give permission without cheerleading.
- Prefer a useful question over a slogan.
- Never imitate a named designer or imply an endorsement.
- Avoid `unlock`, `unleash`, `elevate`, `revolutionary`, `seamless`, and similar generic product language.
- Do not describe breathing or music as treatment, therapy, or a requirement.
- Preserve names, dates, technical constraints, and qualifiers from source files.

For SOMETHINGS-ON, a good line leaves room: `You don't need the whole idea.`
